# Resource Allocation & Pre-positioning

Guidance for allocating and pre-positioning responder units across districts.

## Demand vs Capacity
Each district has a nominal hourly capacity of concurrent units. When forecast demand approaches or exceeds capacity, pre-position units from adjacent districts before the surge arrives. Acting on the forecast rather than the backlog is the difference between a managed response and a queue.

## Pre-positioning Rule
When forecast demand for a district exceeds its capacity within the next hour, move enough units to cover the projected gap plus a one-unit buffer. Draw first from the nearest district that is below its own forecast capacity.

## Prioritization
Prioritize Tier 2+ hazmat and priority-1 medical and fire incidents. Within a developing event, allocate medical capacity proportional to the number of affected-person reports.

## Mutual Aid
Request mutual aid when two or more districts are simultaneously over capacity, or when a single event is projected to exceed citywide reserve for more than one hour.
