# Hazmat Response SOP

Standard operating procedure for hazardous-material and suspected gas-leak incidents.

## Tier Classification
Tier 1 covers a confined, single-source release with no injuries. Tier 2 covers a release with reported symptoms (difficulty breathing, dizziness, nausea) or multiple affected people. Tier 3 covers a release threatening a populated area or critical infrastructure. Suspected natural-gas leaks with multiple citizen reports are treated as Tier 2 until cleared.

## Initial Response
For Tier 2 and above, dispatch a hazmat unit plus at least two medical units and establish an isolation perimeter upwind. High wind (above 20 mph) widens the plume; expand the perimeter and pre-position additional medical capacity downwind.

## Public Alert Trigger
Issue a shelter-in-place advisory when a Tier 2+ release is suspected in a populated area, when three or more correlated citizen reports describe the same hazard within a few blocks, or when responder modeling predicts demand exceeding local capacity. Advise residents to shelter in place, close windows and doors, and avoid the area.

## Escalation
Escalate to the on-call Fire/Hazmat Supervisor for any Tier 2+ incident, any incident with a predicted escalation probability above 0.5, or any event where forecast demand exceeds district capacity within the hour.
